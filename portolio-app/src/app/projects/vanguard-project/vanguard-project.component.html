EB Games Vanguard

UX Design - Web Design - Service Design

As part of a design class this past Fall my team and I were tasked with generating a digital solution that would resolve a real world business problem for a specific client. With this in mind, my group developed the Vanguard rental service for EB games.

Understanding the problem

Based on our analysis, EB games was losing profit from their primary source of revenue: the buying and selling of physical games. Further research revealed that this was largely due to increasing popularity of digital distribution and alternative vendors such as Steam or Amazon who could offer lower price points due to the fact they had purely digital store fronts.

Our proposed solution was a game-rental service, similar to Gamefly and Game Access, which would allow EB games to leverage existing assets, namely their large inventory, international status, and brick-and-mortar stores, to generate additional revenue.

Initial Concept

In our first attempt in designing our digital platform we used Gamefly, Game Access, and Steam as basis for our UI. The result was a functional layout and a color scheme that mimicked generic game e-commerce sites and made us blend into the crowd. As a consequence we were asked by our profs to challenge ourselves and make a real effort to differentiate our interface from our competition’s, even if it meant going against typical game e-commerce convention.

Revision 1 - Transformational Metaphor

In our second attempt we leveraged a new “diamond motif” throughout our site that was inspired by the button layout found on Playstation and Xbox controllers. Our metaphor was utilized in both our navigation of the site, which was broken up into 4 different quadrants that could be navigated using the arrow keys.

Finalization - Consistency across channels

In our most recent attempt we managed to better leverage our metaphor into both the console and mobile channels. This made our apps/site feel more cohesive as a whole. However we feel there is still room for improvement, especially concerning the game details page on our website, which at the moment acts as a pop-up and detracts from the overall composition when contrasted to the rest of the UI.
