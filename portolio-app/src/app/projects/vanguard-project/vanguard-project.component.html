The Brief

  • As part of a design class this past Fall my team and I were tasked with generating a digital solution that would resolve a real world business problem for a specific client. With this in mind, my group developed the Vanguard rental service for EB games.

Understanding the problem

EB games was losing profit from their primary source of revenue: the buying and selling of physical games due to increasing popularity of digital distribution and alternative vendors such as Steam or Amazon.

Our proposed solution was a game-rental service which would allow EB games to leverage existing assets, namely their large inventory, international status, and brick-and-mortar stores, to generate additional revenue.

Initial Concept - Quirky yet Boring

In our first attempt in designing our digital platform we used Gamefly, Google Play Music, and Steam as basis for our UI. The site was functional but we felt it was too generic to differentiate us from the competition.

Revision 1 - Transformational Metaphor: The End of Being Boring

A diamond motif, inspired by the button layout found on Playstation and Xbox controllers, was implemented throughout our site to guide both the visual aesthetic of our site and navigation of the site, which was broken up into 4 different quadrants that could be navigated using the arrow keys.

Revision 1.1 - Addressing User Pain Points

We also made an effort to resolve pain points we discovered in our competitions’ sites and services, namely providing feedback for when games had been added to a customer’s rental queue and providing a more tangible metric for game availability. However, our UI felt inconsistent across our channels, with the console and mobile channels in particular feeling fairly generic and missing our diamond aesthetic.

Finalization - Achieving Consistency Across Channels and Reflecting

In our most recent attempt we managed to better leverage our metaphor into both the console and mobile channels. This made our apps/site feel more cohesive as a whole. However we feel there is still room for improvement, especially concerning the game details page on our website, which at the moment acts as a pop-up and detracts from the over-all composition when contrasted to the rest of the UI. This project, while fairly polished, is still a work in progress and will likely take one or two more iterations until it is deemed fully complete.
