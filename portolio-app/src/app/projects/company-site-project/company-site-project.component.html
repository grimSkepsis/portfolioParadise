The Brief

  • For an upper division web development course my group and I were asked to design and develop an ecommerce site for a fictional company. Our company, Creation Station, was a makerspace company that runs classes and rents high-end tools to empower its patrons to create.

Starting Out

Starting out we compared existing sites from our previous week of work in order to form the basis of our site. Using atomic design principles we proceeded to develop some initial site components and templates along with a site map to guide development.

Coordination Issues

During our first week of collaboration we experienced our first run of issues in the form of coordinating and communicating what each of us was contributing to the git repository.To resolve this we elected to use Trello, a simple task management app, to increase transparency of what work was in progress and what each of us had committed to do.

Design Critique

During our first round of peer feedback we discovered that our site had issues in terms of responsiveness causing certain images/ui elements to become too large/distracting. In order to correct this we elected to add an expanding margin space to limit the size of our content. This helped keep our content centered and more usable as well as more aesthetically pleasing on larger screens.

IE Blues

During our final round of critique we discovered several issues in non-chrome based browsers. With IE being the worst offender by far, content would tend to spill over the boundaries of divs/sections and make the site less professional looking if not unusable. Through further investigation and css tweaking we were able to remove most if not all of these issues in order to get our site to its more finalized and polished state.
